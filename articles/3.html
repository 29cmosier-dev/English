Written by Jack Merrill

Introduction

The internet plays a major role in teen life today. From social media and streaming to online games and learning, it influences how people think, learn, and socialize. It offers many benefits, like helping kids improve memory and attention through video games, providing access to worldwide information, and allowing them to connect with friends worldwide. A study found that kids who play video games more may have better working memory and impulse control (NIH). However, the internet also changes social spaces. Arcades and public hangouts that people used to enjoy are vanishing, and many friendships now develop online. This offers more connection and convenience, but some might feel more isolated. (Den of Geek).

Health Effects

Excessive screen time can affect health. Harvard experts say that spending long hours gaming can cause vision strain, carpal tunnel syndrome, less sleep, and reduced exercise, impacting both the body and mind (Harvard Health). It can also interfere with real-life friendships and lead some teens to rely too much on screens for entertainment or stress relief (Harvard Health).

Balance

Finding balance is essential. People can still enjoy gaming and the internet if they also include exercise, real life interacting, and sleep. Choosing games and apps that do creativity, learning, or teamwork is helpful. Teens should pay attention to how they feel after screen time and try to use the internet in ways that don't negatively affect their mood or health (NIH).

Conclusion

The internet is not entirely good or bad. It can improve skills, create social connections, and offer new learning opportunities. But too much can harm health and social life. The impact really depends on how people use it. With some thought put into what they do, they can take the benefits. (Den of Geek, Harvard Health).
