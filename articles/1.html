The History of Youth Activities

Written by Maxton Marshall

First Recorded Chistian Youth Activities

The first recorded Youth activity was Sunday school back in the mid-1700s and was Started by Robert Raikes in 1780, these were among the first large-scale efforts to teach children, providing religious instruction and literacy skills outside of state-mandated education. The second recorded Christian Youth activity was YMCA (Young Men's Christian Association). Founded in London in 1844, it was an early international organization aimed at providing a safe environment for young men with spiritual, social, and educational activities.

Physical Youth Activities

Another recorded youth activity is Summer Camps. The first recorded summer camp was in 1861, started by Frederick and Abigail Gunn for their students, which included activities like boating and fishing. The second recorded youth activity was the Boys' Brigade. The Boys' Brigade was established in 1883, this organization focused on physical and character development for young men, leading to many similar uniformed youth groups. Working with young woman was seen as less important since woman’s needs in 1500-1877 in this time period were seen mostly at home and in charge of the homemaking/household management. Which was mostly accounted for with the wife since they mainly stayed home and were housewife’s and took care of the kids. This was changed in the year 1878 when youth work pioneer Maude Stanley made work with young woman in the Five Dials area of London. After this she went on and established the Girls Club Union (*1)
