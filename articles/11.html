From Skate Parks to Community Gardens: The Importance of Inclusive Outdoor Spaces for Teens

Written by Jack Merril

Intoduction

Teens really need outdoor spaces where they can be themselves and explore things they like. These can be active places like skate parks, basketball courts, or parkour areas, or quieter spots like community gardens and art corners. Having these spaces helps teens socialize, build confidence, and take breaks from home or school life (Raising Children). (CyberDive).

Active Recreation

Skateparks and sports areas give teens a chance to be active and learn new skills. Skateboarding, BMX, basketball, or parkour lets them challenge themselves and take small risks safely, which helps confidence. Teens meet others who like the same stuff and form friendships that matter (ResearchGate). Without these informal spaces, teens might spend more time inside and miss out on exercise and social connection (ScienceDirect).

Quieter Spaces

Community gardens and art spaces give teens a place to relax, reflect, be creative or other. These spaces let people explore interests at their own pace while learning and expressing themselves (Project for Public Spaces).

Outdoor spaces are best when people get to have a say in them. Letting teens help plan or maintain parks and skateparks gives them ownership and makes them more likely to use spaces positively. Communities that listen to teenagers about what they need, from active spots to quiet corners, see better engagement and safer neighborhoods (Project for Public Spaces; Community Rec Mag).

Conclusion

Teens need outdoor spaces to grow, be active,connect with others. Gardens and art spaces let teens relax and express themselves. Communities that offer a variety of inclusive outdoor spaces help teens develop socially, emotionally, and physically. (Raising Children; CyberDive; PPS; ResearchGate; ScienceDirect; Community Rec Mag).
